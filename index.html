The Horus Heresy

A data-driven dive into loyalty and betrayal.

The Horus Heresy is a book series published by Black Library. Set in the year 30,000, over the course of 64 books it tells the story of the Primarch Horus betraying the Emperor and -- along with 8 of his brothers -- attempting to wrest control of the Imperium for himself.

The Horus Heresy is long, clocking in at about 7,484,862 words. For reference, A Song of Ice and Fire is around 1.8 million words and the Wheel of Time is around 4.4 million words (according to this site). This is split across 64 books (which, for you Chaos conspiracy theorists, is 8*8) for an average of 116,951 words per book. This is also 1,042 average words per perspective, and 10,099 words per character.

There's already some summary stats here, but this time we're diving a lot deeper. In particular, the summary looks at the series overall and focuses on character perspectives. But here, we're going book by book, and we're also analysing every line of dialogue in every book.
